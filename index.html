Assignment 6 « Data Visualization Dashboard »













Note: most of the pie chart implementation was copied from this tutorial from the d3 homepage. I wasn't sure how to change the implementation to make it my own. The legend
came from this tutorial, also from d3. The bar charts came from the lab.




1. The first graph clearly is a bar graph that shows the top titles of the year. The higher-grossing titles are at the top and have the longest bars. As well, the rank of each title
is indicated on the left. There are no duplicate titles. To search a year, click on the button at the bottom of the screen.

Next, I used a bar graph to show the most popular titles for the most common genres for each region. The region can be toggled using the buttons at the bottom. More
common genres are shown at the top, and color is constant for each genre.

Finally, I show the 5 most popular publishers for each genre. Genre can be toggled using buttons at the bottom. The graph contains an “other” section, which is all publishers
not in the top five. Thus, you get a sense of how large the largest publishers are, relative to the entire industry.



2. D3 gave me great control over almost every aspect of the bar graph, from which elements to include (e.g. counts) to font size to color. D3 also nicely automated many
aspects of the bar / pie charts. The existence of helpful tutorials online makes d3 far more useful.



3. Mainly, certain tools are extremely nicely designed for certain visuals. Excel, for example, can help you create simple bar graphs extremely quickly and can also provide a
large number of other tools (such as trendlines) more easily than D3 can.



4. Color-blind users may struggle with the bar graph, since they may struggle to distinguish between sections. The yellow sections (which were created using D3’s default color
scheme) do not contrast well with a white background, making it difficult for users with poor vision. Blind users will not be able to see much of this information. To make it more
accessible to blind people, I could have included a paragraph with a summary of the graph that a screen reader could access.



5. Time was my biggest factor. There also are some tradeoffs, e.g. certain color-blind friendly color schemes may be ugly, certain useful features (like a slider) may be difficult
for people with assistive technology to use, and certain useful features (e.g. visualization in general) may be inaccessible to the blind.
